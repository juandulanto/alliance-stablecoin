How it works

A card that signs its own transactions.

Colossus repurposes standard EMVCo. terminals to sign blockchain transactions. Your cardholder taps. The terminal routes to Colossus instead of a legacy network. Settlement happens on-chain in USD-backed stablecoins.

01
Patented card cryptography.
Each card holds an ECDSA private key in its secure element. A tap generates a signed ERC-4337 transaction. The card is the private key.
02
Non-custodial settlement.
Colossus never holds your funds. Stablecoins move from cardholder to merchant on a public ledger. You, your cardholder, and the merchant all see the same transaction.
03
Fee reduction for merchants.
No assessments. No per-auth fees. No batch fees. No interchange downgrades. Merchants pay the tier rate and nothing else.
The Alliance

The Alliance is modeled on the founding of Visa.

In 1970, Bank of America transferred ownership of BankAmericard to a cooperative of member banks. That cooperative became Visa Inc.

Colossus starts as a company-operated network. The Alliance evolves into its governing body. Members earn equity tied to the processing volume they contribute. Your equity share scales with your processing volume.

FAQ
Is Colossus live?
The network runs on testnet with the full transaction lifecycle validated: card cryptography, authorization, on-chain settlement, and dispute resolution. Commercial launch depends on signing the first merchant acquirer, expected 2027.
What does the MOU commit me to?
Adding Colossus as an additional network on your card programs once Colossus goes live with an acquirer. The commitment is forward-looking, not a current routing obligation.
What about disputes and chargebacks?
Colossus has a full dispute lifecycle with reason codes, evidence windows, and arbitration. The structure mirrors Visa and Mastercard consumer protections, settled on-chain.
Who operates the network today?
Colossus, the company. The Consortium's member-owned governance model activates as the network reaches commercial scale.
Where will transactions settle?
Transactions settle on your respective network. Colossus will manage integration with acquirers and merchants for settlement.
